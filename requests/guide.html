1The system at a glance

Beverley Round Table gives money to local causes as well as raising it. This system runs the giving side, all on our own domain and in our own brand.

Think of it in four parts: the front door (a public form where causes apply), the filing cabinet (a private Google Sheet that stores every application), the back office (a committee-only admin page where we screen, decide and pay), and the shop window (a public page celebrating what we've funded).

There's no separate server to run. The Google Sheet that stores the applications also runs the code behind everything, and the pages are simple files served through Cloudflare. Decisions are always made by our members at the monthly meeting — the system records what was decided, it doesn't decide anything itself.

2The journey of a request

Every application moves through the same sequence. One status drives where it sits in the admin:

Received→ Screening→ Tabled→ Decided→ Paid→ Reported→ Closed
  • Received — the cause has submitted the form; it's waiting to be screened.
  • Screening — an officer checks it's local, eligible and complete (or parks it pending more detail).
  • Tabled — it's ready to put to the members at the next meeting.
  • Decided — the meeting has voted; we record approved, declined or deferred.
  • Paid — the treasurer has paid an approved grant and logged it.
  • Reported — the beneficiary has told us how the money was spent.
  • Closed — the request is complete.

4Guiding the public

Someone wants funding

Send them to the form at donations.beverleyroundtable.co.uk. It helps to tell them:

  • It's for local causes in and around Beverley.
  • Decisions are made by our members at our monthly meeting.
  • They'll hear back by email — and should keep the reference the form gives them.
  • They'll need: who they are, the amount, what it's for, who benefits and roughly how many, and bank details for payment if successful.

After we've funded them

Point them to …/requests/report to tell us how it went. They sign in with their reference and the email they applied with — no account needed.

To show what we do

The public page at …/requests/causes shows the causes we've chosen to feature — good for sponsors, applicants and recruitment.

5The committee process

Signing in

Go to …/requests/admin, enter your committee email, and click the link we email you. It signs you in for 30 minutes. (We currently sign in with the shared committee email — see Running it to give individuals their own logins, which makes the history clearer about who did what.)

New → screen it

Open a new application and check it's local, an eligible cause, and complete. Then:

  • Eligible — tables it for the next meeting.
  • Park — if you need more from the applicant first.
  • Ineligible — closes it (add a reason).

Tabled → the meeting

Tabled requests go to the members at the monthly meeting. After the vote, open the application and use Record the meeting's decision: choose approved, declined or deferred, enter the amount approved, the proposer and seconder, the show-of-hands (for / against / abstain), and — always — the minute reference. Deferred sends it back to Tabled for next time.

Approved → payment

The treasurer pays the grant from the NatWest account, then opens the application and uses Log payment (amount, date, reference). Only do this once the money has actually left the account. Logging a payment automatically clears the applicant's bank details.

6Emailing applicants

Every application has an Email the applicant panel with ready-made templates — acknowledge receipt, request more information, good news, and a kind decline. Pick one, edit it to suit, and send. Messages go out from our Beverley Round Table address, replies come back to us, and every send is recorded in the application's history.

Use the decline template to let people down gently and invite a future application — it's written for that.

7Report-back & the public page

After a grant is paid, point the beneficiary to …/requests/report. Their update lands on the application, which moves to Reported.

Open it and you'll see their report and whether they ticked consent to share. If you'd like to feature it, hit Publish to public page — it then appears on the causes page. To take it down again, use Remove.

What's safe about the public page

  • Nothing appears until an officer hits Publish — applicant consent alone doesn't make it public.
  • It never shows names, emails, phone numbers or bank details — just the cause (the organisation, or "a local good cause" for an individual), what it was for, the amount, and the report-back.
  • The admin warns you if a beneficiary didn't tick consent — check with them before publishing.

8Reconciliation & the dashboard

Reconciliation tab

Shows two lists: grants approved but not yet paid (expected debits) and grants paid (with their references). It's the giving side of the books — match the paid list against the NatWest statement.

Dashboard tab

Headline totals — given to date, causes funded, people helped, average grant, and the share of decided requests funded — plus giving over time and by area. It's all anonymised, so it's safe to show at the AGM, in a grant bid, or to a sponsor.

9Golden rules

  • Don't edit the sheet by hand — always act through the admin, so the audit trail stays intact.
  • Record decisions after the meeting, always with the minute reference.
  • Only log a payment once the money has actually left NatWest.
  • Nothing is public until an officer hits Publish.
  • Keep the sheet shared with the committee only — it holds bank details until a grant is paid.

10Data protection

  • Bank details are deleted automatically the moment a grant is paid.
  • Applicants' personal details are deleted within 12 months of a request being closed; only an anonymised financial record is kept for the accounts.
  • The privacy notice (…/requests/privacy) explains all of this to applicants — keep its link on the form.
  • Only committee members can see applications. The dashboard and the public page never show personal data.

Worth confirming

Whether Beverley Round Table needs to register with the ICO, and a short data-protection impact assessment (DPIA) — sensible because we may handle information about children or vulnerable adults. This guide is practical, not legal advice; a quick check with someone qualified is worthwhile.

11Running it & handover

Where everything lives

  • The pages — GitHub repo BeverleyRoundTable/BRT, in the requests/ folder (index.html is the form, plus privacy, admin, report, causes).
  • The data & code — the "Donation Requests (Responses)" Google Sheet; the code is under Extensions → Apps Script (Config.gs, Setup.gs, Api.gs).
  • Routing — a Cloudflare rule sends the bare donations.beverleyroundtable.co.uk to the form; deeper paths serve the matching page under /requests/.

Common tasks

To…Do this
Add a committee memberIn Config.gs, add their email to ADMIN_ALLOWLIST, save, then redeploy (below).
Make any code change liveIn the Apps Script editor: Deploy → Manage deployments → edit (pencil) → Version: New version → Deploy. The web-app URL stays the same. Pages just need re-uploading to GitHub.
Change the rulesIn Config.gs → POLICY, then redeploy.

Settings (Config.gs → POLICY)

SettingWhat it does
GRANT_CAP_GBPFlags requests above this amount for attention. 0 = no cap.
IN_AREA_PREFIXESFlags out-of-area requests by postcode (e.g. ['HU17','HU16']). Empty = don't flag.
RETENTION_MONTHSHow long applicant data is kept after a request closes. Default 12.

Two other settings live in Config.gs: ADMIN_ALLOWLIST (who can sign in) and AUTH.ADMIN_URL (where the sign-in link points — must match the admin page's address).
